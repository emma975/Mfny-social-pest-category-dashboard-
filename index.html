Unit Share by Category Using Revenue-to-Unit Conversion
% of total units sold. Derived from revenue share / avg price per unit per category. Low-price categories gain share vs revenue view. Hover for sources.
The gap between revenue and unit share reveals which categories punch above or below their weight. Vaporizers command ~25% of revenue but only ~13% of units because avg price ($52-$65) is the highest of any mainstream format. Edibles are the inverse: ~14.5% of revenue but ~21% of units because gummies ($20-$27) and cans ($5) have the lowest per-transaction cost. Pre-rolls are roughly proportional across both measures. Consumption Reason: Medical vs Non-Medical (NYS)
BRFSS 2018-2022. MRTA Implementation Report, Fig 20.
Smoking declined from 76.4% to 69.8% (2018-2022) and is projected to fall to ~55% by 2028 as edibles double from 7% to 22%. Post-legalization (2022): medical-only use dropped to 13.5% (from 19.2% peak in 2020) while non-medical-only rebounded to 52.1%. Multimodal consumption surged - 39% of users reported 2+ modes in 2022, up from 27% in 2021. (MRTA Implementation Report; OCM 2025 Annual Report)

Methodology: Actual category share data from OCM reports and Headset POS tracking. Projected values (P) derived from a demographic-weighted model: Category_Share(t) = Sum[GenWeight(gen,t) x (BasePref(gen,cat) + Drift(gen,cat) x years)] across Gen Z, Millennial, Gen X, and Boomer cohorts. Three interacting forces modeled: (1) demographic weight shift (Gen Z 24% to 33% of total spend), (2) intra-cohort preference drift (BRFSS 2018-2023 mode trends, Headset category YoY), (3) compounding interaction effects.

Taxonomy Note: OCM classifies beverages as a sub-category of Edibles (OCM 2024 Market Report, Fig 22: gummies 84%, beverages 9%, chocolates 5%). In all charts above, beverage revenue is included within the Edibles line. Headset tracks beverages as a standalone top-level category. The Beverage YoY KPI on the Macro Category Mix tab uses the Headset taxonomy (headset.io/markets/new-york, Mar 2026). Beverage sub-category detail is visible in the Sub-Category Deep Dive tab under Edibles. This distinction matters strategically: beverages represent a distinct consumer behavior (alcohol substitution, social/impulse purchase at ~$5/can) that differs fundamentally from gummies ($27 avg, dose-controlled wellness use).
